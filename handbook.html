Parents Handbook

Guidelines and policies to ensure a safe and nurturing environment.

Welcome to Iqra Daycare & Preschool

Our Parents Handbook provides essential information about our policies, procedures, and expectations. We believe that open communication between parents and staff is vital for the success and well-being of your child.

Policies & Procedures

To maintain a safe and nurturing environment, we have established the following policies:


Exclusion of Children Who Are Ill

In compliance with state regulations, children will be excluded from care under the following circumstances:

  • Fever: Any infant with a temperature of 100.4°F or over, or toddlers and preschoolers with a fever of 100°F or higher will be excluded. The child must be fever-free for at least 24 hours without the aid of medication before returning.
  • Diarrhea: Exclusion will occur after two episodes of diarrhea in one day. The child must be diarrhea-free for 24 hours before returning.
  • Vomiting: Exclusion will occur after one episode of vomiting. The child must be vomit-free for 24 hours before returning.
  • Contagious Diseases: Children with contagious diseases (e.g., chickenpox, measles, mumps) will be excluded until a doctor clears them for return (doctor’s note required).
  • Other Conditions: Additional conditions that may lead to exclusion include unexplained rashes, severe coughing, pinkeye, lice, or any illness preventing the child from participating comfortably in daycare activities.

Child Development Program

Our program aims to foster children's cognitive, social, emotional, and physical development through age-appropriate activities and learning centers, including:

  • Free Play Area: Opportunities for free play with educational toys, games, and puzzles to stimulate independent learning at the beginning of the day as we are waiting for everyone to arrive.
  • Library and Literacy Center: Circle Time will be the first activity of the day where the children will learn rhymes and listen to story books with the help of props. This section will also have a front-facing children’s bookshelf if the kids want to read books on their own during the day.
  • Outdoor Play Area: Supervised outdoor activities like running, playing with balls, and exploring nature.
  • Nap Area / Calm Down Zone: Rest times are provided according to each child’s age and needs.
  • Infant Play Area: A supervised play pen with infant-safe toys where babies can learn to play and explore independently.
  • Discovery Centre: Structured and unstructured math and science learning. Mathematical activities will be aligned with fine motor skills milestones.
  • Creative Center: An area which will be used for arts and crafts, and to improve children’s fine motor skills.
  • Gross Motor Area: Indoor physical activity center focused on the physical development of children.
  • Dramatic Play Area: Self-guided learning using toys like play-kitchen, play-workshop, etc.

Diapering Procedures

The following diapering procedures will be enforced:

  • Frequent Changes: Diapers will be checked regularly and changed immediately when wet or soiled.
  • Individual Wipes: Disposable wipes will be used to clean children during diaper changes.
  • Storage and Disposal: Soiled diapers will be placed in a sealed container and disposed of properly.
  • Sanitizing: Diaper-changing surfaces will be sanitized after each use or covered with disposable sheeting.
  • Hand Washing: Proper handwashing will be required after each diaper change to prevent the spread of germs.

Disaster Preparedness Plan

In the event of an emergency, the following procedures will be followed:

    Evacuating and Moving Children to a Safe Location:

    In the event of:

    • Fire: Unlock any washroom doors where kids are present; gather all the kids and leave the facility from the nearest exit; go to the sidewalk at the front of the house while making sure the kids are at my side at all times.
    • Tornado: Gather the kids and go down to the tornado shelter in the basement; keep monitoring the weather report on the phone.
    • Flood: Gather the kids and move to a location farthest away from the flooded area in the facility.
    • Other Natural or Man-Made Disasters: Gather the kids and assess where the safest location for the children will be; safely evacuate the kids to the decided location.
    • Arrangements Made Before Disaster: Prepare a bag with all the safety supplies including first-aid kit, contact information forms, water, snacks, etc. This bag will be stored in an easily-accessible location in case of an evacuation.

    Notifying Parents of Children in Case of an Emergency:

    • Method of Notifying the Parents: Parents will be contacted at the numbers they indicate on the contact information form. In case a parent cannot be reached, the emergency contact numbers will be attempted.
    • Plan if Cell Phones Do Not Work: Contact attempts will be made through a landline phone or through a trusted neighbor’s phone.
    • Arrangements for Care if Parents Cannot Take Children: [Specify arrangements, e.g., staying with trusted family members, local emergency services, etc.]

  • Reunification: Parents can reunite with their children at the designated safe location, communicated to them via phone.
  • Special Needs: At this time, I do not have any special needs children in my care. When and if I do, I will come up with a suitable evacuation and reunification plan accommodating children with special needs.
  • Diagrams: Fire and tornado evacuation routes will be visibly posted in the facility with distinct markings (e.g., red for fire, blue for tornado).
